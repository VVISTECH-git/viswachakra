Cash flow by month — claims raised vs payments received

The rhythm of your cash: purple is what you billed each month, green is money that actually landed. They rarely line up — because the insurer pays old claims in sudden bulk batches, not steadily. Big green with small purple = a backlog being cleared.

Where the claimed money went

Of every rupee you billed, how much actually came back (green), how much the insurer cut as deductions (red), and how much is still stuck with them, unpaid (amber). The whole circle is what you were owed.

Claim outcomes by count

Counting claims (not rupees): how many have been paid, how many are still pending, and how many were rejected or cancelled outright.

Where claims are right now

A live snapshot of every claim's current stage — paid, on hold, under review, sent back, or stuck. The longest bars show where most of your claims are sitting today.

Avg days to get paid — by the month the claim was raised

The real waiting time. Each bar is a month you submitted claims; its height is how many days those claims took to get paid, on average. The older months tell the truth — well over a year. Recent months look shorter only because just the quick ones are paid so far; the rest are still waiting.

Paid vs still-pending — by month raised

For each month you submitted claims, how many have actually been paid (green) versus how many are still waiting (amber). Tall amber columns in recent months = a whole month of your claims the insurer hasn't touched.

How long settled claims took (distribution)

Of the claims that HAVE been paid, how long each one took — sorted into time bands. Bars stacking up on the right (91–180, 180+ days) mean most claims dragged past three to six months before payment.

Avg settlement time by month paid

The same delay seen from the payment side: for claims paid in each month, how many days they'd been waiting on average before the money finally came.

How fast do you get paid?

Of the claims that were settled, the share paid within 3 months (green), in 3–6 months (amber), or dragging past 6 months (red). A big red slice is the headline — most payments take over half a year.

Claims paid per month (count)

How many claims the insurer actually settled each month. Notice the spikes — they clear big batches all at once, then go quiet for months. Steady payment would look flat; this looks like a heartbeat.

Claim size vs time to settle

Every dot is a settled claim: how much it was for (left–right) versus how long it took to get paid (up–down). If the dots drift upward as you go right, bigger claims take longer to pay.

Deduction rate by month (%)

For every ₹100 you claimed, how much the insurer knocked off before paying — as a percentage, month by month. A rising line means they're cutting more of your bills over time.

Rupees deducted per month

Not a percentage — the actual money. This is how many rupees simply vanished to deductions each month: amounts you earned, billed, and never received.

Money stuck by age of claim

Of the money still unpaid, how long it's been waiting. The red bar (90+ days) is your money frozen for over three months — the older it gets, the harder it is to recover.

Money stuck by age (polar view)

The same stuck-money aging, shown as a polar chart — each wedge is a time band, and the further it reaches out, the more money is frozen in that age group. The big outer wedge is your oldest, hardest-to-recover money.

Why claims are stuck (unpaid)

The reasons your unpaid claims are held up — patient feedback pending, queries raised, sent back, and so on. This is your action list: the top bars are what to chase first to unlock payment.

Claims submitted per month

Your workload over time — how many claims (surgeries and treatments) you submitted each month. This is the volume of business flowing through the hospital.

Top procedures by volume

Which surgeries and treatments make up most of your claims — where your clinical work is concentrated, and which procedures matter most to your revenue.

Procedure risk map — speed vs deductions vs volume

Each bubble is a procedure. Further right = slower to get paid; higher up = more heavily deducted; bigger bubble = more claims. The bubbles in the top-right are your problem procedures: high volume, slow to pay, and heavily cut.
